About NDIS

India's Unified Digital
Identity Platform

The National Digital Identity System (NDIS) is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology under the Digital India programme. It provides every Indian citizen with a secure, unified digital identity vault.

NDIS eliminates the need to carry physical documents or stand in queues at government offices. Citizens can store, access, verify, and apply for all government-issued identity documents — from Aadhaar and PAN to Passport and Driving Licence — with military-grade encryption and instant accessibility.

Built with a zero-knowledge architecture, NDIS ensures that only you hold the keys to your data. All access events are immutably logged, providing full transparency and auditability of every interaction with your identity vault.

Eligibility

Who Can Register
on NDIS?

NDIS is available to all Indian citizens and eligible residents. Check if you qualify.

🇮🇳

Indian Citizens

  • Must be an Indian citizen (by birth or naturalisation)
  • Minimum age: 18 years (or guardian registration for minors)
  • Valid Aadhaar card or Aadhaar enrolment number
  • Active mobile number linked to Aadhaar
  • Valid email address for account verification
🌏

NRI & OCI Holders

  • Non-Resident Indians with valid Indian passport
  • Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) cardholders
  • Valid PAN card or previous Indian identity proof
  • International mobile number with +91 alternate
  • Embassy-attested identity documents accepted
👶

Minors (via Guardian)

  • Children below 18 years of age
  • Parent or legal guardian must have an NDIS account
  • Birth certificate required for registration
  • Guardian's Aadhaar linked for verification
  • Account transitions to individual control at age 18
📌

Required Documents: At minimum, you need one primary identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Passport), one address proof (utility bill/bank statement), and a recent passport-size photograph. All documents must be clear, legible scans in PDF, JPG, or PNG format (max 5MB each).

Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions?
We've Got Answers

Find answers to common queries about the National Digital Identity System.

What is the National Digital Identity System (NDIS)?▼
NDIS is a centralised, government-verified platform launched by the Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology under the Digital India programme. It allows Indian citizens to securely store, manage, verify, and apply for all government-issued identity documents in one place — from Aadhaar and PAN to Passport and Driving Licence.
Is NDIS free for all citizens?▼
Yes, NDIS is completely free for all Indian citizens. There are no charges for account creation, document storage, verification, or download. Some document applications (like Passport) may carry standard government processing fees as applicable.
What documents can I store on NDIS?▼
NDIS currently supports Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID (EPIC), Birth Certificate, Driving Licence, Passport, Ration Card, and Caste Certificate. More document types are being added as the platform expands through government agency integrations.
How secure is my data on NDIS?▼
NDIS uses AES-256-GCM encryption for all documents at rest and TLS 1.3 for data in transit. The platform follows a zero-knowledge architecture — only you hold the encryption keys. All systems are ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certified and audited quarterly by CERT-In empanelled agencies. Full audit logs are maintained for every access event.
How long does document verification take?▼
For existing documents linked via Aadhaar OTP or DigiLocker, verification is instant. For new document applications, processing typically takes 3–7 working days depending on the document type and issuing authority. You can track real-time status from your dashboard.
Can I use NDIS from my mobile phone?▼
Yes, NDIS is fully responsive and works on all modern mobile browsers. Additionally, a dedicated mobile app is available with biometric authentication (fingerprint/face ID) support. You can also generate time-limited QR codes for instant identity verification on the go.
What if my document application is rejected?▼
If your application is rejected, you'll receive a detailed notification explaining the reason (e.g., unclear document scan, mismatched information). You can correct the issues and resubmit at no additional cost. Our support team is available 24×7 via the toll-free helpline 1800-111-NDIS for assistance.
How do I contact NDIS support?▼
You can reach us via the 24×7 toll-free helpline at 1800-111-NDIS (6347), email at support@ndis.gov.in, or live chat available Monday–Saturday 9 AM–6 PM IST. For in-person queries, visit the NDIS Help Desk at Electronics Niketan, 6 CGO Complex, Lodhi Road, New Delhi.

System Overview

What is NDIS?

The National Digital Identity System (NDIS) is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), launched under the Digital India programme. It provides every Indian citizen with a secure, unified digital identity vault — a single platform to store, manage, verify, and apply for all government-issued identity documents.

By eliminating the need to carry physical documents and visit government offices, NDIS empowers citizens with instant access to their official identities — from Aadhaar and PAN to Passport and Driving Licence — with the highest standards of data security and privacy.

Security & Privacy

Security and Privacy Commitment

We believe that citizen data sovereignty is non-negotiable. NDIS is built on a "Privacy by Design" framework. Our zero-knowledge architecture ensures that only you hold the encryption keys to your data — government administrators cannot view your unencrypted files.

Documents are encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest and secured with TLS 1.3 in transit. All platform access is protected by multi-factor authentication, including mandatory biometric (fingerprint/face) validation for sensitive actions. Every interaction with your identity vault generates an immutable audit log, providing complete transparency into when and how your data is accessed.

Legal & Compliance

Legal and Compliance Information

NDIS operates strictly within the legal framework established by the Government of India. The platform is governed by the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP) 2023, ensuring your rights as a data principal are fully upheld. It also complies with the Aadhaar Act 2016 and the Information Technology Act 2000.
