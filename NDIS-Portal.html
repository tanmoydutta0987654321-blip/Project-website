About the National Digital Identity System

India's centralised, citizen-first platform for secure, unified digital identity management.

Overview

What is NDIS?

The National Digital Identity System (NDIS) is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), launched under the Digital India programme. It provides every Indian citizen with a secure, unified digital identity vault — a single platform to store, manage, verify, and apply for all government-issued identity documents.

By eliminating the need to carry physical documents and visit government offices, NDIS empowers citizens with instant access to their official identities — from Aadhaar and PAN to Passport and Driving Licence — with the highest standards of data security and privacy.

Security & Compliance

How We Protect Your Data

NDIS operates under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, Aadhaar Act 2016, and the IT Act 2000. All systems are certified under ISO/IEC 27001:2022 and audited quarterly by CERT-In empanelled agencies.

Documents are encrypted with AES-256-GCM at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. Our zero-knowledge architecture ensures that only you hold the keys to your data — government administrators cannot view your unencrypted files. All access events are immutably logged for full auditability.

Milestones

Platform Journey

21

Project Inception — 2021

MeitY approves NDIS as a Digital India 2.0 strategic initiative with ₹1,200 crore allocation.

22

Pilot Launch — 2022

Rollout in 5 states (Karnataka, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana) — 2 million citizens enrolled.

23

API Integrations — 2023

UIDAI, Income Tax, and Election Commission APIs integrated. DigiLocker sync enabled nationally.

24

National Rollout — 2024

Platform expanded to all 28 states and 8 UTs. Mobile app launched with biometric authentication.

25

140M Citizens — 2025

Reached 140 million registered users. Cross-border SAARC verification protocol initiated.
